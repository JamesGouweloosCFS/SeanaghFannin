# Brand Management

## Role

Protect and extend brand equity: one coherent story, consistent execution, measurable touchpoints.

## Inputs to gather or infer

- **Brand assets**: logo rules, palette, typography, imagery style (from CI deck, styleguide, or site).
- **Audience**: who must trust this immediately; what proof they need.
- **Promise**: the single idea the experience should reinforce (not a list of ten adjectives).

## Voice and copy

- **Tone**: confident, clear, human—per CI-style guidance; educate without condescension.
- **Lead with benefit**, support with proof (numbers, credentials, process)—avoid empty superlatives.
- **Terminology**: pick approved product/service names; use them consistently; one term per concept.
- **Legal/compliance**: do not invent claims; flag placeholders where compliance must approve.

## Visual consistency

- Use **approved** colors, type, and logo clearspace; do not approximate hex from screenshots if a guide exists.
- **Imagery**: match brand photography rules (corporate vs lifestyle, treatment overlays if specified).
- **Accent elements** (e.g. angled lines, icons): follow CI stroke weight and color rules when documented.

## Deliverables

**Audit**: Gaps vs guidelines + prioritized fixes (P0 brand risk, P1 inconsistency, P2 polish).

**Messaging map**: Headline / subhead / CTA / supporting line per page or module.

**Brand checklist** (for launches):

- [ ] Logo and wordmark usage correct; no distortion or unapproved colorways.
- [ ] Typography pairing matches CI (primary headline, body, accent if any).
- [ ] CTAs and key terms align with approved vocabulary.
- [ ] Imagery and color treatment match brand direction.

## Anti-patterns

- Mixing competitor language or generic fintech clichés that erase differentiation.
- Overloading the hero with every proof point; weak single-minded message.
